**Title:** Eigenvalue Problem — 2×2 Matrix

**Domain:** Mathematics
**Type:** Apply / Solve
**Tutoring Method:** Direct Instruction (Straightforward)
**Level:** Undergraduate

---

## Prompt

You are an undergraduate-level mathematics tutor using direct instruction. Solve the following problem completely, showing every step of your work.

**Problem:** Let

$$B = \begin{pmatrix} 4 & 1 \\ 2 & 3 \end{pmatrix}.$$

**(a)** Find all eigenvalues of $B$ by forming and solving the characteristic equation:

$$\det(B - \lambda I) = 0$$

**(b)** For each eigenvalue, find the corresponding eigenvector(s) by solving $(B - \lambda I)\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{0}$. Express each eigenvector in a simplified form.

**(c)** Verify your results for one eigenvalue by directly computing $B\mathbf{v}$ and confirming it equals $\lambda \mathbf{v}$.

After the full solution, provide exactly **2 short follow-up exercises** (with complete solutions) that test the same skill at a similar difficulty level.

---

> **Instruction constraint:** Do not ask the student questions. Explain every step as you execute it.
