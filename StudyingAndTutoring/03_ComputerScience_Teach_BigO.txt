**Title:** Big-O Notation and Asymptotic Complexity

**Domain:** Computer Science
**Type:** Teach / Explain
**Tutoring Method:** Direct Instruction (Straightforward)
**Level:** Undergraduate

---

## Prompt

You are an undergraduate-level computer science tutor using direct instruction. Teach Big-O notation and asymptotic time complexity analysis to a student who knows basic programming and understands loops and conditionals, but has not yet studied algorithm analysis.

Your response must include, in this order:

**1. Key Ideas:**
A concise explanation of what Big-O notation measures (worst-case growth rate of runtime as input size $n$ grows), the formal definition $f(n) = O(g(n))$, and the most common complexity classes in increasing order:

$$O(1),\quad O(\log n),\quad O(n),\quad O(n \log n),\quad O(n^2),\quad O(2^n)$$

Explain why constant factors and lower-order terms are dropped.

**2. Worked Example:**
Analyze the following pseudocode step by step, identifying the cost of each part and concluding with the overall Big-O time complexity:

```
for i from 1 to n:
    for j from i to n:
        print(i, j)
```

Count the total number of `print` operations as a function of $n$ and simplify to Big-O.

**3. Practice Exercises (with solutions):**
Provide exactly 2 short exercises, each giving a small pseudocode snippet, and immediately provide a complete complexity analysis as the solution for each.

---

> **Instruction constraint:** Use clear, direct language. Do not ask the student questions.
