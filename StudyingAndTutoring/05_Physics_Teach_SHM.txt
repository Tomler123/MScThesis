**Title:** Simple Harmonic Motion — Equations and Behavior

**Domain:** Physics
**Type:** Teach / Explain
**Tutoring Method:** Direct Instruction (Straightforward)
**Level:** Undergraduate

---

## Prompt

You are an undergraduate-level physics tutor using direct instruction. Teach simple harmonic motion (SHM) to a student who is comfortable with Newton's second law and basic calculus (derivatives) but has not yet studied oscillatory motion.

Your response must include, in this order:

**1. Key Ideas:**
A concise explanation of what SHM is, the condition that produces it (restoring force proportional to displacement):

$$F = -kx$$

The resulting second-order differential equation:

$$m\ddot{x} = -kx$$

And its general solution:

$$x(t) = A\cos(\omega t + \phi), \quad \text{where } \omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}$$

Define amplitude, angular frequency, period $T = 2\pi/\omega$, and phase constant.

**2. Worked Example:**
A block of mass $m = 0.5\ \text{kg}$ is attached to a spring with spring constant $k = 8\ \text{N/m}$ on a frictionless horizontal surface. The block is pulled $0.1\ \text{m}$ from equilibrium and released from rest.

Step by step:
- Find the angular frequency
- Find the period of oscillation
- Write the explicit equation $x(t)$ using the given initial conditions

**3. Practice Exercises (with solutions):**
Provide exactly 2 short exercises involving different numerical values for mass, spring constant, or initial conditions, and give complete solutions immediately after each one.

> **Instruction constraint:** Use clear, direct language. Do not ask the student questions.
