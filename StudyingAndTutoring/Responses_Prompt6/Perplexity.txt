## Complete Solution: SHM Energy and Kinematics
### Part (a): Angular Frequency and Period
The angular frequency of a mass-spring system is derived from Newton's second law:

\(\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} = \sqrt{\frac{50}{0.2}} = \sqrt{250} = 15.81 \text{ rad/s}\)

The period is the reciprocal relationship with angular frequency:

\(T = \frac{2\pi}{\omega} = \frac{2\pi}{15.81} = 0.397 \text{ s}\)
### Part (b): Amplitude Using Energy Conservation
Energy conservation states that the total mechanical energy (kinetic plus potential) remains constant throughout the oscillation:

\(E_{\text{total}} = \frac{1}{2}kx_0^2 + \frac{1}{2}m|\dot{x}(0)|^2 = \frac{1}{2}(50)(0.04)^2 + \frac{1}{2}(0.2)(0.3)^2\)

\(E_{\text{total}} = 0.040 + 0.009 = 0.049 \text{ J}\)

At maximum displacement (amplitude), all energy is potential:

\(\frac{1}{2}kA^2 = 0.049 \implies A = \sqrt{\frac{2(0.049)}{50}} = 0.0443 \text{ m} = 44.3 \text{ mm}\)
### Part (c): Phase Constant and Equation of Motion
The general form of SHM is \(x(t) = A\cos(\omega t + \phi)\), with velocity \(v(t) = -A\omega\sin(\omega t + \phi)\).

Applying initial conditions at \(t = 0\):

**From position:** \(x(0) = A\cos(\phi) = 0.04 \implies \cos(\phi) = \frac{0.04}{0.0443} = 0.9035\)

**From velocity:** \(v(0) = -A\omega\sin(\phi) = -0.3 \implies \sin(\phi) = \frac{-(-0.3)}{(0.0443)(15.81)} = 0.4286\)

Since \(\cos(\phi) > 0\) and \(\sin(\phi) > 0\), the phase angle is in Quadrant I:

\(\phi = \arctan\left(\frac{\sin(\phi)}{\cos(\phi)}\right) = \arctan(0.474) = 0.443 \text{ rad} = 25.4°\)

**Equation of Motion:**

\[x(t) = 0.0443\cos(15.81t + 0.443) \text{ m}\]

**Verification:** \(x(0) = 0.0443 \cos(0.443) = 0.040\) m ✓ and \(v(0) = -(0.0443)(15.81)\sin(0.443) = -0.300\) m/s ✓
### Part (d): Speed at Equilibrium
At equilibrium (\(x = 0\)), all energy is kinetic:

\(\frac{1}{2}mv_{\text{eq}}^2 = E_{\text{total}} \implies v_{\text{eq}} = \sqrt{\frac{2(0.049)}{0.2}} = \sqrt{0.49} = 0.70 \text{ m/s}\)

Alternatively, \(v_{\text{eq}} = A\omega = (0.0443)(15.81) = 0.70\) m/s ✓
