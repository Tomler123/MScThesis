### Exercise 1

**Problem:** Let \(C = \begin{pmatrix} 5 & -2 \\ 1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\). Find all eigenvalues and corresponding eigenvectors.

**Solution:**

Form the characteristic equation:

\[C - \lambda I = \begin{pmatrix} 5-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 2-\lambda \end{pmatrix}\]

\[\det(C - \lambda I) = (5-\lambda)(2-\lambda) - (-2)(1) = 10 - 5\lambda - 2\lambda + \lambda^2 + 2 = \lambda^2 - 7\lambda + 12\]

\[\lambda^2 - 7\lambda + 12 = (\lambda - 3)(\lambda - 4) = 0\]

**Eigenvalues:** \(\lambda_1 = 4\) and \(\lambda_2 = 3\)

For \(\lambda_1 = 4\):

\[\begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & -2 \end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} v_1 \\ v_2 \end{pmatrix} = \mathbf{0} \implies v_1 = 2v_2\]

**Eigenvector:** \(\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\)

For \(\lambda_2 = 3\):

\[\begin{pmatrix} 2 & -2 \\ 1 & -1 \end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} v_1 \\ v_2 \end{pmatrix} = \mathbf{0} \implies v_1 = v_2\]

**Eigenvector:** \(\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\)

### Exercise 2

**Problem:** Let \(D = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 3 \\ 0 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\). Find all eigenvalues and corresponding eigenvectors.

**Solution:**

Form the characteristic equation:

\[D - \lambda I = \begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & 3 \\ 0 & 2-\lambda \end{pmatrix}\]

\[\det(D - \lambda I) = (1-\lambda)(2-\lambda) - 0 = (1-\lambda)(2-\lambda)\]

**Eigenvalues:** \(\lambda_1 = 1\) and \(\lambda_2 = 2\)

For \(\lambda_1 = 1\):

\[\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} v_1 \\ v_2 \end{pmatrix} = \mathbf{0} \implies 3v_2 = 0 \text{ and } v_2 = 0\]

**Eigenvector:** \(\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}\)

For \(\lambda_2 = 2\):

\[\begin{pmatrix} -1 & 3 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} v_1 \\ v_2 \end{pmatrix} = \mathbf{0} \implies -v_1 + 3v_2 = 0 \implies v_1 = 3v_2\]

**Eigenvector:** \(\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\)
