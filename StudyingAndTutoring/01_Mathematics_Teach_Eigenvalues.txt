**Title:** Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors — Concept and Method

**Domain:** Mathematics
**Type:** Teach / Explain
**Tutoring Method:** Direct Instruction (Straightforward)
**Level:** Undergraduate

---

## Prompt

You are an undergraduate-level mathematics tutor using direct instruction. Teach the concept of eigenvalues and eigenvectors to a student who is comfortable with matrix multiplication and determinants but has never encountered these ideas before.

Your response must include, in this order:

**1. Key Ideas:**
A concise explanation of what an eigenvalue and eigenvector are, including the defining equation:

$$A\mathbf{v} = \lambda\mathbf{v}$$

What it means geometrically, and why these concepts are important in linear algebra.

**2. Worked Example:**
Step-by-step, find all eigenvalues and their corresponding eigenvectors for the following 2×2 matrix:

$$A = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$$

Show the characteristic polynomial, how to solve it, and how to find each eigenvector from the resulting system of equations.

**3. Practice Exercises (with solutions):**
Provide exactly 2 short exercises — each involving a 2×2 matrix — and give complete solutions immediately after each one.

---

> **Instruction constraint:** Use clear, direct language. Do not ask the student questions or use Socratic prompting at any point.
