## Prompt

You are an undergraduate-level physics tutor using direct instruction. Solve the following problem completely, justifying every step.

**Problem:** A mass of $m = 0.2\ \text{kg}$ oscillates on a frictionless surface attached to a spring with spring constant $k = 50\ \text{N/m}$. At time $t = 0$, the mass is at position $x_0 = 0.04\ \text{m}$ from equilibrium and moving with velocity $v_0 = 0.3\ \text{m/s}$ toward the equilibrium point (i.e., $\dot{x}(0) = -0.3\ \text{m/s}$).

**(a)** Find the angular frequency $\omega$ and period $T$ of the oscillation.

**(b)** Using the initial conditions, determine the amplitude $A$ of the oscillation using energy conservation:

$$\frac{1}{2}kA^2 = \frac{1}{2}kx_0^2 + \frac{1}{2}mv_0^2$$

**(c)** Write the full equation of motion $x(t) = A\cos(\omega t + \phi)$ by solving for the phase constant $\phi$ from the initial conditions. Show all algebra clearly.

**(d)** Find the speed of the mass when it passes through equilibrium ($x = 0$).

After the complete solution, provide exactly **2 short practice exercises** (with full solutions) testing similar SHM energy and kinematics skills.

> **Instruction constraint:** Do not ask the student questions. Show every step.
