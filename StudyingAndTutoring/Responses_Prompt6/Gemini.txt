### **Solution to SHM Energy and Kinematics Problem**

**Given Values:**
* Mass ($m$): **0.2 kg**
* Spring constant ($k$): **50 N/m**
* Initial position ($x_0$): **0.04 m**
* Initial velocity ($v_0$): **-0.3 m/s** (The negative sign indicates movement toward equilibrium from a positive position).

---

#### **(a) Angular Frequency and Period**
The angular frequency $\omega$ of a mass-spring system depends only on the mass and the spring constant.

1.  **Angular Frequency ($\omega$):**
    $$\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}$$
    $$\omega = \sqrt{\frac{50}{0.2}} = \sqrt{250} \approx 15.81\text{ rad/s}$$

2.  **Period ($T$):**
    The period is the time it takes to complete one full cycle. 
    $$T = \frac{2\pi}{\omega}$$
    $$T = \frac{2\pi}{\sqrt{250}} \approx 0.397\text{ s}$$

**Answer:** The angular frequency is **15.81 rad/s** and the period is **0.397 s**.

---

#### **(b) Amplitude Using Energy Conservation**
The total mechanical energy $E$ of the system is the sum of its potential and kinetic energy at any given moment. At maximum displacement (the amplitude, $A$), all energy is potential. 

1.  **Calculate Total Energy ($E$) at $t = 0$:**
    $$E = \frac{1}{2}kx_0^2 + \frac{1}{2}mv_0^2$$
    $$E = \frac{1}{2}(50)(0.04)^2 + \frac{1}{2}(0.2)(-0.3)^2$$
    $$E = 25(0.0016) + 0.1(0.09)$$
    $$E = 0.04 + 0.009 = 0.049\text{ J}$$

2.  **Solve for Amplitude ($A$):**
    Set the total energy equal to the maximum potential energy:
    $$E = \frac{1}{2}kA^2$$
    $$0.049 = \frac{1}{2}(50)A^2$$
    $$0.049 = 25A^2$$
    $$A^2 = \frac{0.049}{25} = 0.00196$$
    $$A = \sqrt{0.00196} \approx 0.0443\text{ m}$$

**Answer:** The amplitude of the oscillation is **0.0443 m** (or **4.43 cm**).

---

#### **(c) Equation of Motion**
The general equation of motion for position is $x(t) = A\cos(\omega t + \phi)$. To find the phase constant $\phi$, we use the initial conditions at $t = 0$.

1.  **Position at $t = 0$:**
    $$x(0) = A\cos(\phi) = 0.04$$

2.  **Velocity at $t = 0$:**
    Taking the derivative of $x(t)$ gives the velocity function: $v(t) = -A\omega\sin(\omega t + \phi)$.
    $$v(0) = -A\omega\sin(\phi) = -0.3$$

3.  **Solve for $\phi$:**
    Divide the velocity equation by the position equation to isolate $\tan(\phi)$:
    $$\frac{-A\omega\sin(\phi)}{A\cos(\phi)} = \frac{-0.3}{0.04}$$
    $$-\omega\tan(\phi) = -7.5$$
    $$\tan(\phi) = \frac{7.5}{\omega} = \frac{7.5}{\sqrt{250}} \approx 0.4743$$

    To determine the correct quadrant for $\phi$, look at the signs of sine and cosine from steps 1 and 2:
    * $\cos(\phi) = \frac{0.04}{A}$ (Positive)
    * $\sin(\phi) = \frac{0.3}{A\omega}$ (Positive)
    
    Since both are positive, $\phi$ is in the first quadrant.
    $$\phi = \arctan(0.4743) \approx 0.443\text{ rad}$$

**Answer:** The full equation of motion is **$x(t) = 0.0443\cos(15.81t + 0.443)$**, where $x$ is in meters and $t$ is in seconds.

---

#### **(d) Speed at Equilibrium**
When the mass passes through the equilibrium point ($x = 0$), all of its potential energy has been converted to kinetic energy. This is where it reaches its maximum speed ($v_{\text{max}}$).

1.  **Using Kinematics:**
    $$v_{\text{max}} = A\omega$$
    $$v_{\text{max}} = \sqrt{0.00196} \cdot \sqrt{250} = \sqrt{0.49} = 0.7\text{ m/s}$$

    *(Alternatively, using energy conservation: $\frac{1}{2}mv_{\text{max}}^2 = E \rightarrow 0.1v_{\text{max}}^2 = 0.049 \rightarrow v_{\text{max}} = 0.7\text{ m/s}$)*

**Answer:** The speed of the mass when it passes through equilibrium is **0.7 m/s**.

### **Practice Exercises**

**Exercise 1: Maximum Velocity from Energy**
A **0.5 kg** mass oscillates on a frictionless surface attached to a spring with a spring constant of **200 N/m**. The total mechanical energy of the system is **4 J**. Calculate the amplitude of the motion and the maximum speed of the mass.

**Solution 1:**
1.  **Find Amplitude:** $$E = \frac{1}{2}kA^2$$
    $$4 = \frac{1}{2}(200)A^2$$
    $$4 = 100A^2 \implies A^2 = 0.04 \implies A = 0.2\text{ m}$$
2.  **Find Maximum Speed:** Maximum speed occurs when all energy is kinetic.
    $$E = \frac{1}{2}mv_{\text{max}}^2$$
    $$4 = \frac{1}{2}(0.5)v_{\text{max}}^2$$
    $$4 = 0.25v_{\text{max}}^2 \implies v_{\text{max}}^2 = 16 \implies v_{\text{max}} = 4\text{ m/s}$$
*(Final Answer: $A$ = **0.2 m**, $v_{\text{max}}$ = **4 m/s**)*

---

**Exercise 2: Determining the Phase Constant**
A simple harmonic oscillator has an amplitude of **5 cm**. At $t = 0$, its position is $x = -2.5\text{ cm}$ and it is moving in the positive x-direction. Find the phase constant $\phi$ assuming the equation of motion is $x(t) = A\cos(\omega t + \phi)$.

**Solution 2:**
1.  **Evaluate Position at $t = 0$:**
    $$x(0) = A\cos(\phi)$$
    $$-2.5 = 5\cos(\phi) \implies \cos(\phi) = -0.5$$
    This places $\phi$ in either the 2nd quadrant ($\frac{2\pi}{3}$) or 3rd quadrant ($\frac{4\pi}{3}$).
2.  **Evaluate Velocity at $t = 0$:**
    $$v(0) = -A\omega\sin(\phi)$$
    We are told the mass is moving in the positive x-direction, so $v(0) > 0$. Since $A$ and $\omega$ are always positive, $-A\omega\sin(\phi)$ can only be positive if $\sin(\phi)$ is negative. 
3.  **Determine Quadrant:**
    Cosine is negative and Sine is negative. Therefore, $\phi$ must be in the 3rd quadrant.
    $$\phi = \frac{4\pi}{3}\text{ rad}$$ 
*(Final Answer: $\phi$ = **$\frac{4\pi}{3}$ rad**)*
