### 1. Key Ideas
SHM occurs when a restoring force is proportional to displacement: $F = -kx$.
The resulting differential equation is $m\ddot{x} = -kx$, with the general solution:
$x(t) = A\cos(\omega t + \phi), \quad \text{where } \omega = \sqrt{k/m}$
*   **Amplitude ($A$):** Maximum displacement from equilibrium.
*   **Angular Frequency ($\omega$):** Rate of oscillation (rad/s).
*   **Period ($T$):** Time for one full cycle, $T = 2\pi/\omega$.
*   **Phase Constant ($\phi$):** Determines the starting position at $t=0$.

### 2. Worked Example
For $m = 0.5\text{ kg}$, $k = 8\text{ N/m}$, $x(0) = 0.1\text{ m}$, $v(0) = 0$:
*   **Angular frequency:** $\omega = \sqrt{8/0.5} = 4\text{ rad/s}$.
*   **Period:** $T = 2\pi/4 = \pi/2 \approx 1.57\text{ s}$.
*   **Equation:** Since it starts at max displacement, $\phi = 0$ and $A = 0.1$. Thus, $x(t) = 0.1\cos(4t)$.

### 3. Practice Exercises

    **Solution:** $\omega = \sqrt{100/1} = 10\text{ rad/s}$.
